TULSI

CLASSIFICATION

  1. Scientific Name:Holy Basil
  2. Kingdom: Plantae
  3. Phylum: Spermatophyta
  4. Class: Magnoliopsida
  5. Order: Sanctum or tenuiflorum
  6. Family: Lamiaceae
  7. Genus: Ocimum

DESCRIPTION

Tulsi, also known as Holy Basil, is a perennial herb or shrub Tulsi is a small, aromatic, and much branched shrub with hairy stems and simple, toothed leaves. The leaves can be green or purple, depending on the variety. The flowers are small, tubular, and purple or white, with green or purple sepals. Tulsi can grow up to 1 meter (3.3 feet) in height. Tulsi is native to India and Southeast Asia Tulsi can grow in full sun or partial shade, but it produces more oil when grown in full sun. Tulsi needs regular watering, but avoid overwatering to prevent root rot. Tulsi grows well in long days and high temperatures. Tulsi flourishes well in humid conditions and fairly high rainfall. Tulsi can be attacked by pests like mealy bugs, aphids, spider mites, and whiteflies. You can treat pests with a neem-based organic pesticide or insecticidal soap. Regular pruning promotes bushy growth. Protect the plants from frost during colder months

PROPERTIES AND USES

Tulsi contains antioxidants like eugenol that protect the heart from free radicals. Tulsi has anti-inflammatory properties that can help treat colds, coughs, and respiratory infections. Tulsi can help normalize blood sugar imbalances. Tulsi can help normalize blood pressure imbalances. Tulsi may help boost the immune system. Tulsi may help improve digestion. Tulsi may help reduce stress levels. Tulsi has insecticidal activity against mosquitoes. Tulsi contains vitamins A and C, as well as calcium, zinc, and iron. Tulsi may have a positive effect on several metabolic processes, including improving LDL cholesterol. Tulsi is important in traditional medicine. Tulsi can be consumed in various forms, such as tea, capsules, and even as a seasoning in food.

PRECAUTIONS

May affect reproductive health; consult a doctor before use. Can increase bleeding risk; avoid if on blood thinners. Lowers blood sugar; monitor levels if diabetic. Prolonged use may reduce male fertility. May cause allergic reactions; discontinue if symptoms appear. Can interfere with thyroid, blood pressure, or heart medications. May cause stomach upset or nausea in high doses. Use cautiously and under medical advice.
